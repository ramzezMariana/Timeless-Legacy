About the Project

This collection highlights important figures like George Washington, Helen Keller, and Abraham Lincoln through museum artifacts—like busts, life masks, and fossils—that offer a personal glimpse into their lives. By exploring these objects, we connect with the people behind the history, gaining insight into their impact and legacy. Each artifact shows a different side of the figure, making history feel more tangible and meaningful.

Artifacts

George Washington Marble Bust

Created around 1819 by Massimiliano Ravenna after Giuseppe Ceracchi's original, honors America's first president as a soldier, statesman, and foundational figure in democracy.

Learn More

Captain Gabriel Archer Burial

Excavated in 2017 at Historic Jamestowne, provide a glimpse into the lives and struggles of the early Jamestown colonists. These bones, buried in the church chancel, connect us to the resilience of the settlers who helped lay the foundations of America.

Learn More

Australopithecus africanus: Cranium

Discovered in Sterkfontein, South Africa, provides key insights into a pivotal stage of human evolution. Dating back 2.8 to 2.4 million years, it highlights early bipedalism, a small brain, and a mixed diet, offering a vital link to our evolutionary past.

Learn More

Helen Keller Life Mask

Created in 1916, captures the spirit of a remarkable advocate who defied being deaf and blind to inspire generations. This artifact symbolizes Keller's strength, determination, and enduring legacy as a writer and humanitarian.

Learn More

Abraham Lincoln Life Mask

Created in 1860 by Leonard Wells Volk, offers a rare glimpse of the future president just before his pivotal role in American history. This plaster mask, cast in 1917, captures Lincoln's likeness before the Civil War's toll and serves as a timeless reminder of his leadership in shaping America's future.
